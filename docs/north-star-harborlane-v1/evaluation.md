## What worked

- The page reads as a real customer-facing offer, not a documentation page or internal product shell.
- The asymmetric hero and large sprint artifact panel create a stronger first-read and clearer premium positioning.
- CTA hierarchy is clear across hero, pricing offer card, and final CTA band.
- Every major section has a designed visual surface (problem visuals, process timeline, bento tiles, transformation, proof).
- Desktop and mobile both preserve hierarchy and avoid collapsing into plain text blocks.

## What is still weak

- The visual anchor is strong but still mostly panel-based; a more signature brand-level artifact could improve memorability.
- Interaction states are refined but conservative; more distinctive motion choreography could increase perceived premium quality.
- The trust modules are credible but could use one deeper proof artifact (for example, a richer mini case card stack) to feel truly flagship.

## Benchmark verdict

**Decision: revise**

This is a high-quality benchmark candidate and materially stronger than a generic SaaS/docs layout, but it does not yet feel final "north-star definitive." It should be iterated once more for a more iconic hero artifact and a slightly more distinctive interaction signature before being treated as the visual benchmark standard.
