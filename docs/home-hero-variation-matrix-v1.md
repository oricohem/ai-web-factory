# Home Hero Variation Matrix v1 (AI Web Factory)

## Scope and constraints

- Deliverable type: docs + one standalone HTML gallery only.
- Production app remains untouched (`app/`, `components/`, `globals.css`, routes, data, `package.json`, dependencies, auth, backend, and `.env` unchanged).
- Objective: force meaningful structural diversity beyond the previous split-hero bias.

## Gallery file

- `docs/home-hero-variation-matrix/index.html`

## Matrix framework

5 composition types:
1. Centered hero
2. Split left text / right visual
3. Fullscreen visual background
4. Editorial asymmetric layout
5. Product artifact floating stage

4 visual anchor types:
- A. AI particle/core
- B. 3D object/product artifact
- C. Cinematic photo/material scene
- D. Abstract gradient/light field

Total combinations: **20 unique pairings**.

Hard-rule check:
- No duplicate composition + anchor pairing appears in the gallery.

## Top 5 concepts

1. **C5B — Floating Artifact Theater (9.2)**
2. **C3C — Immersive Material Storyframe (9.1)**
3. **C4B — Editorial Object Ledger (9.0)**
4. **C3B — Immersive Artifact Drift (8.9)**
5. **C5A — Floating Core Stage (8.9)**

### Why these are visually stronger

- They combine high compositional distinctness with high anchor clarity; each has immediate first-read hierarchy plus a memorable hero center of gravity.
- They avoid generic template rhythm by using either full immersive background logic, editorial asymmetry, or explicit floating-stage geometry.
- Their anchors are legible as product or production metaphors rather than decorative UI effects.

### Why these avoid dashboard/admin feeling

- None lead with dense modules, sidebars, panel grids, or equal-weight cards.
- Proof and product cues are implicit support layers, not the dominant structure.
- They prioritize landing-grade narrative and visual memory over operational-density framing.

### Why these could become AI Web Factory signature directions

- C5B and C5A give an ownable “launch-stage” visual system tied to AI production identity.
- C3C and C3B offer premium campaign-level distinctiveness while preserving product relevance.
- C4B introduces a branded editorial asymmetry that still feels systemized and commercially credible.

## Top 3 concepts

1. **C5B — Floating Artifact Theater (9.2)**
2. **C3C — Immersive Material Storyframe (9.1)**
3. **C4B — Editorial Object Ledger (9.0)**

### Why these are stronger than the rest

- They balance memorability and trust better than purely atmospheric options.
- They are structurally distinct from each other (floating stage vs fullscreen immersive vs editorial asymmetric), giving strategic optionality.
- They preserve clear CTA hierarchy while maintaining strong premium tone.

### Why these avoid admin-tool regression

- No card-wall dependency.
- No control-panel-first composition.
- No weak black-glow dashboard fallback.

### Signature direction potential

- C5B is most ownable as a branded hero system.
- C3C is strongest for premium visual storytelling.
- C4B is strongest for a distinctive product-marketing voice that still feels software-native.

## Winner to expand next

### **Winner: C5B — Floating Artifact Theater**

#### Why it is visually strongest
- Highest taste score in the matrix.
- Most coherent combination of premium visual theater and productized anchor.
- Strong hierarchy: headline and CTA stay clear while the floating artifact becomes the memory trigger.

#### Why it avoids dashboard/admin feeling
- The composition centers on staged product presence rather than operational panel density.
- It uses artifact-first storytelling, not metrics-first framing.
- It avoids sidebars, dashboards, and nested card language entirely.

#### Why it could become AI Web Factory’s signature
- “Floating production artifact stage” can scale into a repeatable brand motif across homepage, campaigns, and proof sections.
- It aligns with AI Web Factory as a premium production system shipping approved assets.
- It differentiates from generic SaaS gradients by pairing stage geometry with purposeful anchor semantics.

## Honest self-critique

- The matrix intentionally prioritizes breadth over polish, so several mid-scoring concepts are directional sketches rather than implementation-ready visual systems.
- Some concepts still use familiar premium-landing vocabulary; uniqueness will depend on stricter brand language and motion signatures in the next focused pass.
- Scores are comparative within this matrix and should not be interpreted as production quality guarantees without deeper route-level prototyping.
