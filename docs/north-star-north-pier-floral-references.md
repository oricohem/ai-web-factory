# North Star Reference Brief: North Pier Floral Studio

## 1) Purpose

This document is a narrow build brief for one North Star experiment: testing whether the constrained Mira workflow can transfer from boutique ecommerce into a boutique local service/studio business.

It intentionally focuses on a single page-direction decision system (references -> visual family -> iconic moment selection gate -> acceptance checks) so the next build can be judged on transfer quality, not exploration breadth.

## 2) Reference scope

Only these three references are in scope for this experiment:

1. https://soilandstemclasses.thinkific.com/
2. https://movementlabus.com/
3. https://www.bostontattoo.com/

Why this scope is intentionally narrow:

- It follows the Mira learning that exactly three aligned references reduce style drift and generic blending.
- It isolates the transfer test to local service/studio warmth and authored personality, rather than broadening into unrelated visual families.
- It avoids collage behavior from mixed-category inputs and keeps evaluation grounded in one coherent boutique local direction.

Reference-use rule:

- Use references as principle inputs only.
- Do not copy code, layouts, brand language, imagery, trademarks, or assets.

## 3) Reference cards

### Reference 1: Soil and Stem Classes

- **What to learn from it:** Floral/botanical atmosphere cues, natural material context, and workshop/studio class feeling.
- **What not to copy:** Course-platform layout structure, photography style, class naming language, and any branded presentation patterns.
- **How it should influence North Pier Floral Studio:** Push North Pier toward an intimate, studio-led floral world where making/arranging feels tangible and local.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Botanical texture-led background zones, gentle editorial rhythm, and crafted workshop-context components.
- **What risk to avoid:** Drifting into generic educational/course-template composition instead of authored boutique studio identity.

### Reference 2: Movement Lab US

- **What to learn from it:** Human local-service rhythm, approachable warmth, and appointment-led business energy.
- **What not to copy:** Exact service flow structure, section choreography, or local-brand copy cadence.
- **How it should influence North Pier Floral Studio:** Make booking and inquiry feel like entering a trusted neighborhood studio relationship, not a transactional funnel.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Service-first narrative sequencing, clear booking intent moments, and person-centered trust placement early in the page.
- **What risk to avoid:** Over-softening into vague lifestyle mood with weak service clarity or unclear conversion path.

### Reference 3: Boston Tattoo Company

- **What to learn from it:** Authored local personality, confident boutique character, and non-corporate brand voice.
- **What not to copy:** Distinct edge styling, exact typography attitude, brand-specific tonal language, and visual motifs.
- **How it should influence North Pier Floral Studio:** Give North Pier stronger authored confidence so it feels like a real studio with point of view, not a neutral template florist.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Boldly intentional typographic hierarchy, local credibility cues, and visual moments with character rather than generic polish.
- **What risk to avoid:** Swinging too far into aggressive aesthetic language that breaks North Pier's warm botanical tone.

## 4) North Pier brand brief

### Studio profile

- **Business:** North Pier Floral Studio
- **Type:** Small coastal floral design studio
- **Services:** Seasonal arrangements, intimate event florals, weekend flower workshops

### Primary offer

- **Harbor Table Arrangement:** A seasonal floral centerpiece built from coastal textures, soft stems, and sculptural branch forms

### Audience

- Design-aware local customers
- Couples planning small events
- Boutique hospitality owners
- People who want seasonal floral pieces that feel personal, not mass-produced

### Mood

- Coastal
- Natural
- Tactile
- Poetic
- Intimate
- Seasonal
- Studio-made
- Warm but not cute

### Palette direction

- Warm ivory
- Sea-salt white
- Muted sage
- Driftwood brown
- Soft petal blush
- Charcoal ink
- Tiny accent of deep berry or marigold

### Business goal and CTA architecture

- **Business goal:** Book workshop seats, sell seasonal arrangements, and encourage event inquiry requests
- **Primary CTA:** Book a workshop
- **Secondary CTA:** Explore seasonal florals

### Core page promise

Bring the feeling of a coastal garden table into one thoughtful arrangement.

### Required emotional read

- **Must feel:** local, tactile, botanical, warm, authored, seasonal, studio-led, customer-facing
- **Must not feel:** SaaS, ecommerce template, corporate service page, generic wedding vendor, sterile wellness site, documentation/prototype, stock-photo florist site

## 5) Required visual family

**Selected family:** Boutique Local Brand (Botanical Studio Atmosphere variant)

Why this family is selected:

- It best fits an appointment-and-workshop-led coastal floral studio where personality and trust must feel local and authored.
- It preserves warm editorial character while keeping service conversion explicit (booking workshops, inquiry-ready flow).
- It aligns with the selected references' shared strengths: place-based credibility, human craft atmosphere, and boutique confidence without corporate framing.

## 6) Background strategy

Required background behavior for the next North Pier build:

- The hero background must be botanical/studio-specific, not decorative.
- Background cues must connect to flowers, stems, workshop tables, coastal textures, or seasonal arrangement-making.
- The background should reinforce offer meaning (crafted studio florals), not operate as a generic atmosphere layer.
- Generic gradients, generic texture overlays, and stock-floral wallpaper behavior are disallowed.
- Contrast protection for headline/CTA must be built into the background treatment so atmosphere does not reduce clarity.

## 7) Iconic moment requirement

Mandatory pre-build gate for future coding:

- Before any implementation work, the build agent must propose exactly three possible iconic visual moments.
- The agent must wait for explicit user selection before coding.
- The selected moment must appear in the hero or first major section.
- The selected moment must be specific to North Pier's floral/studio story and cannot be a generic landing-page effect.

Five example iconic moment directions (examples only; do not pre-select one yet):

1. Overhead workshop table composition made from arrangement elements and stem/flower silhouettes with tactile paper, twine, and vessel cues.
2. Coastal stem map where floral components are positioned like a studio planning board for one Harbor Table Arrangement.
3. Bouquet-building layer sequence that reads like an editorial floral recipe with seasonal texture transitions.
4. Tide-line floral shelf scene with stem clusters, vessel, handwritten note card, and soft directional shadows as the hero anchor.
5. Seasonal bloom calendar wall interpreted as a tactile studio artifact board (material, stem, and vessel relationships), not a process diagram.

## 8) North Pier-specific 10-point visual acceptance checklist

1. Does the background tell the floral/studio story rather than merely decorate the page?
2. Is there at least one clear botanical or tactile material cue (for example stem texture, vessel surface, paper/wrap, branch structure)?
3. Does the palette read as intentionally coastal-floral (not generic beige or washed-neutral default)?
4. Does typography feel authored and local rather than startup/SaaS marketing defaults?
5. Is there exactly one screenshot-worthy iconic visual moment in the hero or first major section?
6. Is there a real service/workshop/event inquiry flow with clear booking intent, not only mood imagery?
7. Could this be mistaken for SaaS within 1.5 seconds? If yes, fail.
8. Do section transitions feel editorial/studio-led instead of checklist-like template blocks?
9. Does CTA language feel like booking a local studio experience (not generic conversion language)?
10. Does copy sound like a real floral studio voice rather than a landing-page generator output?

## 9) Failure signs

Classic and subtle failure signs to reject in the next North Pier build:

1. Centered generic hero with interchangeable headline/subheadline rhythm.
2. Generic floral stock-photo feeling without studio specificity.
3. Three equal service cards as the main design idea.
4. Template copy such as "beautiful arrangements for every occasion."
5. Gradient background used as main visual identity instead of botanical/studio atmosphere.
6. Icons beside service bullets as primary visual mechanism.
7. Startup-style social-proof language that feels imported from SaaS.
8. Warm colors present, but page rhythm still reads B2B/corporate.
9. No asymmetry or no grid-breaking authored moment.
10. Overall result looks like a mockup/prototype, not a real local brand.
11. Page feels ecommerce-first instead of appointment/service studio-first.
12. Page feels like a generic wedding vendor template instead of authored floral studio identity.

## 10) Required next-step instruction

Before building North Pier Floral Studio, propose 3 iconic visual moments and wait for user selection. Do not code until one is selected.
