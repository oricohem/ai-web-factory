## What worked

- Harbor Ledger composition is clear: utility masthead, booking-first hero flow, operational worktable band, and explicit service-state sections.
- Structural stem + ink colors carry separators, chips, table rules, and control surfaces rather than acting as tiny accents.
- Trust model is local service evidence (hours, address, response window, phone, partners) rather than premium-number claims.
- Final CTA is an operational dock (`Check openings`, `Request event date`) and does not mirror hero prose.

## What is weak

- Hero and service sections are strong, but the process snapshot could use one more distinctive visual interruption to raise memorability.
- Worktable abstraction is intentionally CSS-only; it communicates studio mechanics but is less rich than a bespoke art-directed illustration would be.
- Some tertiary zones still lean neutral and can be pushed further toward stem/slate dominance for even harder divergence.

## Cross-comparison against Mira

- North Pier v2 uses single-flow operational hero grammar; Mira used copy-plus-artifact split grammar.
- North Pier v2 uses rail and ledger sections with hard separators; Mira relied on warm rounded-shell storytelling modules.
- North Pier v2 trust signals are local logistics (hours/location/response/phone); Mira used boutique proof framing.
- North Pier v2 closes with an action dock for immediate operations; Mira-style mirrored promise closure is intentionally absent.
