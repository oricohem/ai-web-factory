# North Star References Brief: Mira Home Objects

## 1) Purpose

This document is a **narrow build brief** for one Mira Home Objects North Star benchmark experiment.  
It is not a universal design system, not a broad style inventory, and not a multi-brand reference map.  
Its role is to force one coherent boutique ecommerce direction so the next build avoids style blending.

## 2) Reference Scope

This brief intentionally uses only three references:

1. https://littlekarmaco.com/
2. https://nohma.com/
3. https://www.bostontattoo.com/

Why this narrow scope:

- Mira needs a specific boutique ecommerce atmosphere, not a mixed collage of AI/SaaS, dashboard, or multi-category patterns.
- `littlekarmaco.com` and `nohma.com` anchor premium tactile commerce behavior.
- `bostontattoo.com` adds local/boutique personality and warmth so Mira does not become sterile or corporate.
- Broader packs are intentionally excluded for this build to reduce drift, overfitting to conflicting aesthetics, and generic template outputs.

Rule: these references are principle inputs only. Do not copy code, layouts, brand language, or assets.

## 3) Reference Cards

### 3.1 Little Karma Co

- **What to learn from it:** Boutique ecommerce polish, tactile product atmosphere, curated product-first framing.
- **What not to copy:** Exact merchandising structure, copy phrasing, composition signatures, imagery treatment.
- **How it should influence Mira:** Push Mira toward warm editorial commerce where products feel intentional and touchable.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Product-led hero, material-aware surfaces, selective whitespace, and restrained conversion hierarchy.
- **What risk to avoid:** Turning into a generic pretty store grid with weak brand story.

### 3.2 NOHMA

- **What to learn from it:** Refined premium product-world presentation with visual restraint and confidence.
- **What not to copy:** Distinctive brand motifs, exact hero choreography, proprietary art direction.
- **How it should influence Mira:** Keep calm premium framing while preserving product specificity around ceramic ritual objects.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Strong headline-to-product relationship, controlled palette, and high-quality product narrative modules.
- **What risk to avoid:** Over-minimal execution that feels sterile, expensive-but-empty, or emotionally flat.

### 3.3 Boston Tattoo Company

- **What to learn from it:** Local/boutique personality, authored character, and less-corporate warmth.
- **What not to copy:** Brand edge, typography signatures, thematic style cues, or service-site composition.
- **How it should influence Mira:** Add human, believable brand tone so Mira feels like a real studio-led object brand.
- **What visual behavior it suggests:** Personality-forward moments, confidence in voice, and non-generic brand rhythm.
- **What risk to avoid:** Drifting into service-business layout logic instead of ecommerce product discovery and purchase intent.

## 4) Mira Brand Brief

- **Brand:** Mira Home Objects
- **Offer:** Boutique ecommerce brand selling small-batch ceramic candle vessels, incense holders, and sculptural home scent objects.
- **Primary product:** The **Dune Vessel** — a hand-poured ceramic candle vessel with a soft sand-colored glaze and refillable scent insert.
- **Audience:** Design-aware women and men, 28-45, apartment/small-home living, buying objects that make spaces calmer, warmer, and more intentional.
- **Mood:** Slow living, warm minimalism, tactile calm, quiet luxury, handcrafted but not rustic.
- **Palette direction:** Warm off-white, sand, clay, soft stone, muted charcoal, tiny accent of deep olive or burnt amber.
- **Business goal:** Sell the first collection drop and collect email signups for limited restocks.
- **Primary CTA:** Shop the first drop
- **Secondary CTA:** View the scent ritual
- **Core page promise:** Turn a shelf, table, or bedside corner into a small daily ritual.

Non-negotiable feel:

- tactile
- warm
- boutique
- real brand
- product-specific
- atmospheric
- ecommerce-ready

Must not feel like SaaS, dashboard UI, generic templates, startup landing page, AI product page, documentation/prototype, or sterile minimalist catalog.

## 5) Required Visual Family

**Selected family:** **Warm Editorial Commerce** (with Boutique Product Atmosphere behavior).

Why this family is selected:

- It best matches Mira’s product type: tactile, small-batch home objects that depend on material and mood cues.
- It supports premium conversion without defaulting to corporate or SaaS design language.
- It allows restrained elegance while preserving emotional warmth and brand specificity.
- It keeps the page ecommerce-ready with curated product narrative, not just abstract lifestyle imagery.

## 6) Background Strategy (Required)

The hero background must be **product-specific**, not decorative.

Required behavior:

- Background should feel like a tactile surface or product world connected to ceramic/scent/ritual.
- Surface logic should imply glaze, clay, shelf, stone, shadow, scent haze, or ritual placement context.
- Background must support legible headline and CTAs through controlled contrast zones.

Forbidden behavior:

- Generic gradients as the primary hero idea.
- Atmosphere unrelated to product semantics.
- Abstract “nice” backdrop with no ritual/material story.

## 7) Iconic Moment Requirement

Before any future coding, the build agent must:

1. Propose **3 possible iconic visual moments** specific to Mira.
2. Wait for user selection.
3. Implement only the selected moment in the hero or first major section.

The iconic moment must be directly tied to Mira’s product story and must create one screenshot-worthy brand memory.

### Five example iconic moment directions (examples only; do not preselect)

1. **Ceramic silhouette + scent rings:** Dune Vessel silhouette with layered shadow and subtle scent-ring geometry that reads as ritual, not tech.
2. **Shelf composition hero:** Asymmetric shelf-like stage with product object cards and cast shadows, showing collection context.
3. **Scent plume integration:** Fine scent-plume or orbit-line motif emerging from the vessel and integrating with headline/CTA region.
4. **Glaze texture panel:** Tactile glaze-texture field behind hero copy with protected contrast windows and product cutout framing.
5. **Ritual display timeline:** Three-step ritual display presented as product arrangement sequence, not process diagram UI.

## 8) Mira-Specific Visual Acceptance Checklist (10 Checks)

1. Does the background tell the product story rather than decorate?
2. Is there at least one tactile material cue (glaze, clay, stone, or scent atmosphere)?
3. Does the palette feel selected for Mira, not generic boutique beige?
4. Does typography support a calm premium atmosphere with clear hierarchy?
5. Is there one screenshot-worthy iconic visual moment?
6. Is there a real product/collection section beyond the hero?
7. Could this be mistaken for SaaS in 1.5 seconds? If yes, fail.
8. Do section transitions feel editorial/brand-led rather than checklist-like blocks?
9. Does the CTA language feel like buying/discovering a product, not “converting users”?
10. Does the copy sound like a brand voice, not a landing-page generator?

## 9) Failure Signs (Classic + Subtle)

- Three equal product cards are used as the main design idea.
- Centered generic hero with interchangeable startup structure.
- “Crafted with care” template copy appears without product specificity.
- Gradient background substitutes for tactile/product atmosphere.
- Icons beside feature bullets become the dominant visual language.
- Startup social-proof language appears detached from Mira’s boutique context.
- Page looks nice but could be mistaken for SaaS.
- Warm colors are present, but rhythm still feels B2B/checklist-driven.
- No asymmetry or grid-breaking moment appears.
- Final output looks like a mockup/prototype rather than a real brand.
- Product story is secondary to abstract visual effects.
- CTA hierarchy reads like lead-gen funnel language instead of commerce intent.

## 10) Required Next-Step Instruction

Before building Mira, propose 3 iconic visual moments and wait for user selection. Do not code until one is selected.
