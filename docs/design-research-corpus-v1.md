# AI Web Factory Design Research Corpus v1

Date: 2026-05-03

## Purpose

AI Web Factory needs future visual directions that come from many successful website principles, not one repeated internal style family. This corpus gives agents a reusable research base for generating original, high-quality full-site directions across many categories.

This is a research and workflow document only. It does not approve copying any website, branded asset, screenshot, layout, illustration, motion system, or copyrighted content.

## Method and constraints

- Live web research was available for this pass.
- Research was used only to extract principles: composition, hierarchy, visual anchors, proof, CTA behavior, atmosphere, typography, and common failure modes.
- No assets, screenshots, code, copy, brand identifiers, or proprietary designs were copied into the product.
- The requested hero matrix docs were not present in `docs/` at the time of writing, so this corpus aligns with the available local design guidance instead: `docs/design-learning-report-v1.md`, `docs/design-skills.md`, `docs/modern-premium-visual-concepts-v2.md`, and `docs/visual-identity-v1.md`.
- This corpus expands the existing Taste Engine. It does not replace `ai-factory-taste-engine`, `ai-factory-design-intelligence`, or visual QA requirements for production UI work.

## Core problem to solve

Recent AI Web Factory hero explorations improved quality, but many concepts still share the same family:

- dark premium
- geometric object
- technical grid
- large typography
- product artifact stage

Those are useful genes, not a complete design universe. Future agents must learn from multiple categories and combine principles into original directions that fit AI Web Factory's product story.

## Reference categories

### 1. Modern AI product landing pages

**Why effective:** They answer "what is this, who is it for, and why trust it" quickly. Strong pages lead with outcome clarity rather than model jargon.

**Hero structures:** Outcome headline, concise subhead, one primary CTA, product output or capability proof near the fold.

**Visual anchors:** Product-generated artifact, before/after example, workflow preview, intelligent assistant surface, or result gallery.

**Typography behavior:** Large direct headline, short supporting copy, labels that explain product value without buzzwords.

**Background / atmosphere:** Clean gradient fields, soft technical depth, or minimal product-stage backdrops that protect readability.

**CTA patterns:** One primary conversion action with optional secondary demo or examples link.

**Trust / proof:** Customer logos, user counts, quantified outcomes, security statements, output examples, or credible benchmark snippets.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Outcome-first messaging, generated website artifact proof, side-by-side input-to-output story, and visible review confidence.

**Must not copy:** Exact AI assistant layouts, brand gradients, prompt/chat motifs, logos, claims, or recognizable product screenshots.

**Failure modes:** "AI-powered" as the whole value proposition, generic chat box heroes, abstract orbs, meaningless automation diagrams, and unsupported metrics.

### 2. Premium SaaS / B2B product sites

**Why effective:** They sell confidence, reliability, and operational value with restrained polish.

**Hero structures:** Positioning headline, buyer-specific value prop, product visual, proof row, and clear enterprise-safe CTA.

**Visual anchors:** Product tour panel, workflow dashboard crop, customer outcome module, or integrated platform diagram.

**Typography behavior:** High-legibility sans, strong hierarchy, short sections, confident but not theatrical scale.

**Background / atmosphere:** Quiet white, ink, slate, or warm neutral systems with disciplined accent use.

**CTA patterns:** "Book demo," "Start trial," "See platform," or "View customer story" after anxiety-reducing proof.

**Trust / proof:** Logos with context, security/compliance cues, case studies, quantified ROI, integrations, and clear pricing logic.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Enterprise trust rails, platform capability map, conversion-led proof, and operational credibility.

**Must not copy:** Logo walls, exact dashboard compositions, pricing copy, compliance badges, or brand color systems.

**Failure modes:** Generic SaaS gradient hero, random logo wall, fake enterprise metrics, over-sanitized sameness, and decorative screenshots.

### 3. Creator tools and no-code tools

**Why effective:** They reduce perceived effort and make creation feel fast, approachable, and empowering.

**Hero structures:** "Create X without Y" headline, visual builder artifact, templates/examples proof, and low-friction CTA.

**Visual anchors:** Canvas, block editor, template gallery, publish flow, or transformation from blank state to finished asset.

**Typography behavior:** Friendly clarity, benefit-led statements, scannable bullets, and direct action language.

**Background / atmosphere:** Light or warm neutral fields, playful accents, simple surface layering, and approachable whitespace.

**CTA patterns:** Start building, use a template, see examples, or watch a short demo.

**Trust / proof:** Creator outcomes, template counts, time-to-publish claims, testimonials, community examples.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Creation flow, component assembly, template-to-site narrative, and low-friction proof of output quality.

**Must not copy:** Specific builder UIs, template thumbnails, community screenshots, creator testimonials, or brand illustration systems.

**Failure modes:** Toy-like UI, too many playful colors, vague "build anything" promises, and no proof that output is premium.

### 4. Luxury / premium product websites

**Why effective:** They create value through restraint, material quality, space, and confidence.

**Hero structures:** Sparse product statement, large image/object, generous negative space, minimal navigation, and slow reveal of detail.

**Visual anchors:** Material close-up, sculptural product image, editorial object crop, or tactile surface.

**Typography behavior:** Elegant scale contrast, limited type families, strong line-height discipline, and deliberate pacing.

**Background / atmosphere:** Matte neutrals, ivory, charcoal, soft grain, refined shadows, tactile texture, and controlled color.

**CTA patterns:** Understated but precise actions, often secondary to product desire and story.

**Trust / proof:** Craft narrative, provenance, quality markers, press quotes, and curated testimonials.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Material restraint, artifact quality, premium whitespace, and confidence in fewer elements.

**Must not copy:** Product photography, luxury brand layouts, type pairings tied to known brands, or exclusivity language.

**Failure modes:** Empty luxury with no product clarity, decorative serif misuse, slow low-information pages, and fake scarcity.

### 5. Architecture / interiors / spatial websites

**Why effective:** They organize experience through space, scale, light, and calm sequencing.

**Hero structures:** Full-bleed spatial image, asymmetrical text block, project-style story entry, or room-like sectional flow.

**Visual anchors:** Spatial frame, horizon, material wall, plan-like structure, or environmental perspective.

**Typography behavior:** Quiet editorial hierarchy, large titles, compact captions, and generous text-image spacing.

**Background / atmosphere:** Light direction, natural material fields, subtle texture, image-led negative space, and room-like depth.

**CTA patterns:** View project, explore process, schedule consultation, or see details.

**Trust / proof:** Case studies, process documentation, before/after, material details, client quotes.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** A "production environment" metaphor, spatial pipeline rooms, artifact galleries, and calm full-site pacing.

**Must not copy:** Interior imagery, architectural project layouts, floor-plan motifs, or branded studio language.

**Failure modes:** Pretty spaces unrelated to product, over-literal factory rooms, low-contrast type over images, and slow gallery bloat.

### 6. Editorial / magazine-style websites

**Why effective:** They create hierarchy, rhythm, and authority through typography and layout variety.

**Hero structures:** Asymmetric grid, feature story, large headline, secondary story rail, author/proof metadata, or image-led spread.

**Visual anchors:** Headline itself, editorial image crop, pull quote, issue-style module, or content index.

**Typography behavior:** Strong display scale, readable body measure, captions, labels, metadata, and rhythm between dense and open sections.

**Background / atmosphere:** Paper-like neutrals, high contrast, single accent color, structured grids, and strong whitespace.

**CTA patterns:** Read, explore, subscribe, view report, or inspect a feature.

**Trust / proof:** Author bylines, publication rhythm, citations, issue labels, editorial standards, and curated archives.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Editorial intelligence workspace, narrative sections, proof reports, and stronger content hierarchy.

**Must not copy:** Magazine brands, article layouts, photography, byline styles, or exact grid systems.

**Failure modes:** Newspaper cosplay, too many text styles, poor mobile wrapping, and editorial drama that hides product action.

### 7. Agency / portfolio websites

**Why effective:** They create memory through art direction, typography, transitions, and selective case proof.

**Hero structures:** Large typographic statement, interactive canvas, selected work preview, showreel, or manifesto plus case-study path.

**Visual anchors:** Expressive type, motion behavior, project thumbnails, cursor-reactive surface, or unusual grid.

**Typography behavior:** Big confident headlines, kinetic but readable type, strong labels, and fewer words.

**Background / atmosphere:** High-contrast fields, monochrome systems, image masks, motion-ready layers, and brand-led texture.

**CTA patterns:** View work, start project, see case studies, or contact.

**Trust / proof:** Case outcomes, client names, awards, featured work, process explanations.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Memorable direction naming, case-study style output proof, bolder hierarchy, and motion-ready section pacing.

**Must not copy:** Portfolio pieces, agency signatures, motion concepts tied to a studio, award marks, or client lists.

**Failure modes:** Style over product, inaccessible motion, over-clever navigation, generic agency black-and-white layouts.

### 8. Cinematic / entertainment / gaming-inspired sites

**Why effective:** They sell atmosphere, anticipation, and narrative through scene design.

**Hero structures:** Cinematic hook, trailer-like visual field, short story line, platform/action row, and progressive scene sections.

**Visual anchors:** Scene still, character/object silhouette, lighting event, environment, or animated world layer.

**Typography behavior:** High-impact title type, short emotional copy, supporting labels, and clear action despite atmosphere.

**Background / atmosphere:** Directional lighting, depth haze, vignette, color grading, scene transitions, and dramatic contrast.

**CTA patterns:** Watch, join, wishlist, play, explore, or enter experience.

**Trust / proof:** Press quotes, community metrics, creator pedigree, launch dates, platform availability.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Cinematic production metaphor, scene-like section sequencing, dramatic but restrained proof reveals.

**Must not copy:** Game assets, film stills, trailers, genre iconography, character silhouettes, or entertainment brand palettes.

**Failure modes:** Sci-fi cliché, random particles, unreadable drama, over-dark pages, and gaming energy that reduces enterprise trust.

### 9. Fintech / trust-heavy product sites

**Why effective:** They reduce anxiety before asking for action.

**Hero structures:** Credible claim, legitimacy proof, plain-language security explanation, and calm CTA.

**Visual anchors:** Trust module, account flow, compliance rail, transparent pricing/fee card, or verification diagram.

**Typography behavior:** Clean, precise, conservative hierarchy with strong readability and few tricks.

**Background / atmosphere:** Calm light or deep neutral surfaces, minimal color, stable grids, and low-motion transitions.

**CTA patterns:** Open account, see pricing, talk to expert, or verify eligibility after trust cues.

**Trust / proof:** Regulatory alignment, partner institutions, security standards, transparent fees, client volume, and support access.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Auditability, review confidence, provenance proof, security-minded product language, and decision transparency.

**Must not copy:** Regulatory badges, bank partner names, finance metaphors, guarantee claims, or compliance copy.

**Failure modes:** Overpromising trust, hiding details, flashy motion near sensitive claims, and sterile pages with no brand memory.

### 10. Minimal Apple-like product pages

**Why effective:** They make the product feel inevitable through clarity, depth, precision, and pacing.

**Hero structures:** One product idea, polished product visual, simple headline/subhead, subtle CTAs, and sequential feature sections.

**Visual anchors:** Product object, device-like surface, close-up detail, comparison moment, or feature animation.

**Typography behavior:** Clear hierarchy, precise spacing, strong line-height, minimal type families, and calm copy.

**Background / atmosphere:** Clean white, black, or gentle gradients with exact content protection and strong product focus.

**CTA patterns:** Learn more, buy/start, compare, or watch film, usually minimal and consistent.

**Trust / proof:** Product quality, ecosystem logic, feature demos, technical specs, and customer familiarity.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Section-by-section clarity, product artifact focus, disciplined spacing, and confidence without clutter.

**Must not copy:** Apple layouts, device compositions, type styling, spec-table patterns, animation sequences, or copy tone.

**Failure modes:** Sterile imitation, huge whitespace with weak content, product object without product meaning, and too little conversion proof.

### 11. Photo-led landing pages

**Why effective:** They create immediate emotional or contextual understanding when images are specific and meaningful.

**Hero structures:** Full-bleed or split photo, headline overlay or adjacent copy, primary CTA, and human/product context.

**Visual anchors:** Real user context, product-in-use scene, before/after image, or outcome environment.

**Typography behavior:** High contrast, short benefit headline, restrained overlay text, and clear hierarchy protected by scrims.

**Background / atmosphere:** Image-led, with gradient scrims, masks, or tonal zones for readability.

**CTA patterns:** Strong contrast button, secondary "see examples" or "watch demo."

**Trust / proof:** Authentic imagery, testimonials, visual before/after, customer examples, and contextual proof.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Output-gallery storytelling, photographed-feeling artifact scenes, human review moments, and real use context.

**Must not copy:** Stock photos, identifiable people, photographer styles, image compositions, or brand photo treatments.

**Failure modes:** Decorative stock photo, unreadable overlays, no product connection, and emotional imagery replacing evidence.

### 12. 3D object / product visualization pages

**Why effective:** They reduce uncertainty and create a tangible sense of product value.

**Hero structures:** Large object stage, product label, CTA, supporting feature callouts, and optional interaction affordance.

**Visual anchors:** Interactive object, exploded view, material close-up, configurator, or process/object hybrid.

**Typography behavior:** Minimal labels around the object, strong headline, precise callouts, and restrained copy.

**Background / atmosphere:** Studio lighting, clean gradients, shadows, reflective surfaces, or neutral stage fields.

**CTA patterns:** Explore, configure, view demo, rotate, inspect, or start.

**Trust / proof:** Detail fidelity, dimensions/specs, material evidence, configurability, and comparison support.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** AI production artifact as object, component assembly visualization, pipeline artifact model, and semantic 3D-readiness.

**Must not copy:** Product models, lighting setups tied to brands, object silhouettes, material packs, or configurator UI.

**Failure modes:** Random 3D blob, object with no product meaning, heavy performance cost, and mobile-hostile interaction.

### 13. Gradient / atmospheric modern launch pages

**Why effective:** They create immediacy and brand energy with a lightweight visual system.

**Hero structures:** Centered or split headline, atmospheric color field, simple CTA, and minimal proof artifact.

**Visual anchors:** Gradient field, luminous horizon, aura around product proof, or soft color geometry.

**Typography behavior:** Bold simple headline, high contrast copy, sparse metadata, and minimal sections.

**Background / atmosphere:** Mesh gradients, radial light, subtle noise, blurred color, or animated color shifts.

**CTA patterns:** One bright action button and one subdued secondary link.

**Trust / proof:** Early adopter counts, waitlist momentum, press mention, demo artifact, or launch timeline.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Launch energy, color as mood, lightweight hero experiments, and atmospheric section transitions.

**Must not copy:** Recognizable gradient palettes, glass templates, launch copy, or exact glow compositions.

**Failure modes:** Generic SaaS gradients, muddy contrast, too many color fields, weak product specificity, and glow as a substitute for design.

### 14. Marketplace / platform landing pages

**Why effective:** They clarify supply, demand, and trust quickly.

**Hero structures:** Marketplace value proposition, category/discovery entry, trust guarantee, proof wall, and two-sided explanation.

**Visual anchors:** Category grid, curated inventory, verified badge system, search/discovery module, or proof wall.

**Typography behavior:** Direct utility language, strong filters/categories, short reassurance copy, and clear labels.

**Background / atmosphere:** Clean product grids, approachable surfaces, and predictable navigation.

**CTA patterns:** Search, browse, list, join, get matched, or request access.

**Trust / proof:** Verified participants, guarantees, ratings, buyer/seller protection, transaction volume, curated quality.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Component marketplace logic, verified artifact wall, reusable asset trust, and quality filters.

**Must not copy:** Marketplace category taxonomies, badge designs, inventory imagery, guarantees, or review systems.

**Failure modes:** Card wall, too many categories, unclear two-sided value, weak verification, and generic platform claims.

### 15. Developer tool websites

**Why effective:** They earn trust with specificity, documentation, code, and performance evidence.

**Hero structures:** Clear promise, install/API action, code example or interactive artifact, trust block, and docs path.

**Visual anchors:** Code block, terminal command, API response, architecture diagram, live sandbox, or docs excerpt.

**Typography behavior:** Clean sans with readable mono, high contrast code, short claims, and exact technical labels.

**Background / atmosphere:** Minimal dark or light systems, syntax-aware surfaces, precise dividers, and low decoration.

**CTA patterns:** Get API key, install, read docs, try playground, or view GitHub.

**Trust / proof:** GitHub stars, docs quality, latency metrics, customer quotes, open-source credibility, API examples.

**AI Web Factory can adapt:** Developer-grade proof, component/pipeline examples, command-style CTAs, docs-adjacent clarity, and measurable quality claims.

**Must not copy:** Terminal themes, code samples from real products, docs layouts, repo metrics, or CLI commands.

**Failure modes:** Fake code, over-terminalized brand, unreadable code blocks, marketing before proof, and developer alienation through empty hype.

## Reference-to-Originality Rule

For every reference principle used, future agents must transform it across at least 3 dimensions:

1. **Different product story:** Translate the principle into AI Web Factory's component, pipeline, review, artifact, or website-generation story.
2. **Different layout structure:** Do not keep the same spatial arrangement, section sequence, or above-the-fold composition.
3. **Different visual anchor or material treatment:** Change the anchor, medium, texture, object, image strategy, or proof device.

Recommended extra transformations:

- Change mood register.
- Change density level.
- Change trust strategy.
- Change responsive behavior.
- Change CTA hierarchy.

If a direction can be recognized as a specific reference after swapping the logo, reject it.

## Full Website Generation Workflow

Future agents creating a full website direction must:

1. Define product positioning.
2. Choose target audience.
3. Choose 2-3 reference categories from this corpus.
4. Extract design genes from those categories.
5. Generate 3 materially different full-site art directions.
6. Score each direction and choose one.
7. Create homepage structure.
8. Create full page sections.
9. Define responsive behavior.
10. Run visual QA when implementing UI.
11. Self-critique against anti-patterns.
12. Revise before approval.

For docs-only direction work, do not run screenshots or video capture unless explicitly requested. For production UI work, follow the project QA rules in `AGENTS.md` and `docs/design-skills.md`.

## Scoring Expansion

Use this scoring in addition to the Taste Engine score. Rate each dimension from 0 to 1.

1. **Originality:** Does it avoid recognizable reference copying and same-family repetition?
2. **Category fit:** Do selected categories match the product story and audience?
3. **Brand memory:** Would someone remember this as AI Web Factory, not generic AI SaaS?
4. **Full-site scalability:** Can the system support hero, proof, features, trust, and conversion sections?
5. **Section diversity:** Do sections vary in rhythm without becoming incoherent?
6. **Visual system durability:** Can the direction survive implementation, content changes, and future pages?
7. **Commercial conversion potential:** Does the page guide trust and action?
8. **Implementation realism:** Can it be built without risky dependencies, inaccessible motion, or excessive asset needs?
9. **Responsive quality:** Does it have a credible mobile behavior, not only a desktop poster?
10. **Anti-repetition strength:** Is it materially different from recent dark geometric/product-object directions?

Decision thresholds:

- Below 7.0: reject.
- 7.0-8.0: promising but revise.
- 8.0-8.7: viable if Taste Engine score also passes.
- 8.8+: strong candidate for implementation planning.

## Future Agent Usage Protocol

Future agents must:

1. Read this corpus before major website generation work.
2. Select at least 2 different reference categories.
3. Select at least 8 design genes.
4. Explain the chosen gene combination.
5. Generate at least 3 materially different full-site directions before implementation.
6. Reject directions that are same-family repeats.
7. Only implement after a direction passes scoring and critique.

Recommended output format before implementation:

- Product positioning
- Target audience
- Selected categories
- Selected genes
- Three direction briefs
- Scores and rejection notes
- Chosen direction
- Reference-to-originality transformations
- Anti-patterns avoided
- Responsive strategy
- Proof and QA plan

## How this prevents repeated same-family designs

This corpus forces agents to start from category diversity and design genes instead of style adjectives. It separates reusable principles from visual imitation, requires multi-dimensional transformation, and scores anti-repetition explicitly. A future direction cannot pass by being another dark premium geometric object on a technical grid unless that combination is intentionally selected, transformed, and clearly stronger than materially different alternatives.
